## How it was produced
1. **8 parallel deep-dive research agents** (live 2025–2026 web research) → structured per-sector theses.
2. A **thematic-ETF landscape** support agent.
3. **4 adversarial red-team agents** attacking the theses for crowding, double-counting, timing, substitution, and policy dependence.
4. **Cross-cutting synthesis** of the recurring meta-scarcities, followed by **a final adversarial pass on the synthesis itself**, then revision until critiques stopped landing material hits.

*Method caveat: WebFetch was frequently blocked (HTTP 403) in-environment, so figures rest on cross-checked WebSearch extractions (load-bearing numbers corroborated across ≥2 sources). Vendor roadmap dates and single-source projections are flagged lower-confidence throughout; a few second-hand figures are flagged for primary verification before sizing positions.*

## The one-paragraph conclusion
The research-driven answer to "where is the puck going" is *already* a crowded, richly-priced consensus, because you are asking at the top of the cycle that created the scarcity — yet refusing to own a secular grid/copper/power deficit because it "already ran" is just as costly. Nearly every scarcity (transformers, copper, magnets, gallium, HBM, cooling, turbines) is downstream of **one factor: AI-datacenter capex + electrification**, so a "diversified scarcity basket" is one bet with ~1.0 internal correlation. The durable edge is the **slow-to-build inputs** — electrons/power, grid, skilled labor, processing/separation, proprietary data, single-source materials — not the fast-ramp manufacturing scarcities being actively quadrupled. Two laws keep you safe: a chokepoint is not a rent (Wolfspeed went bankrupt owning one), and the best chokepoints are inaccessible (private/foreign/impaired) — so for the most robust scarcities there is no clean ETF. The single most actionable idea: **own the electrons** (GE Vernova, Siemens Energy, and merchant-power IPPs) — the purest slow-to-build scarcity and a booked backlog that pays you *through* the digestion everyone fears.
