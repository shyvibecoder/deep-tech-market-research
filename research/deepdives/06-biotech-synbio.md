# Biotech & Synthetic Biology — Deep Dive (Wave 1)

**Core thesis:** The binding constraint is NOT the model and increasingly NOT the molecule — it is proprietary wet-lab data, physical delivery/manufacturing capacity, and regulatory-throughput-gated time. Biology refuses to be parallelized; trials and GMP capacity are the real chokepoints. Timing is structurally slower than compute/semis.

## Roadmap
- 2027 H1: First in-vivo CRISPR approval (Intellia lonvo-z, HAE) — landmark (systemic in-vivo).
- 2026–28: First AI-designed molecules reach pivotal data (~200+ AI-designed programs in clinic); none approved yet.
- 2026: $100 genome commoditized (Ultima, Element VITARI, Roche SBX) — sequencing NO longer the bottleneck.
- 2028–29: MCED diagnostics reimbursement unlocks (Medicare, law passed) — but Galleri MISSED in large study Feb 2026 (caution).
- 2026–28: Longevity preclinical→clinical (Retro, Rubedo, BioAge, Insilico) — Phase 1 safety only.
- 2027–30: AI-drug clinical verdict (do AI drugs beat ~90% failure rate?). Early signal mixed (Recursion, BenevolentAI flops).
- 2030–36 speculative: precision fermentation at price parity (needs ~1000× capacity — likely missed), longevity efficacy.

## Structural scarcities
- **A. Proprietary AI-ready biological data + wet-lab validation capacity** — binds NOW, compounds. Moat very durable (data+workflows+capital). Roche 3,500-Blackwell "Lab-in-the-Loop", Lilly LillyPod, Anthropic $400M bio bet = voting with capex. Expressions: Recursion (phenomics), lab automation (Tecan, Thermo, Danaher), Twist; pre-IPO Xaira, Isomorphic, Chai, Retro.
- **B. GMP viral-vector & plasmid manufacturing** — physical chokepoint, binds now, eases slowly 2027–2030. AAV empty capsids 50–90% of batches; affinity resins short. Expressions: Lonza, Catalent, Charles River, OXB, Thermo; Cytiva/Danaher (resins).
- **C. Delivery IP — LNP & AAV** — legal/IP chokepoint. Concentrated in Acuitas, Genevant, Arbutus, Moderna, BioNTech; eases as patents expire early-mid 2030s. Expressions: Arbutus, Genevant (private), Acuitas (private).
- **D. Regulatory/clinical throughput** — irreducible time scarcity; AI can't compress trials/enrollment/FDA review. Permanent. Favors incumbents+CROs; headwind for pure-AI-discovery valuations.
- **E. Bioreactor/fermentation capacity (synbio)** — needs ~1000× by 2030; binds 2026–2032. Expressions: Sartorius, Cytiva/Danaher, Thermo (bioreactor OEMs).

## Non-consensus
1. Proprietary biological data > models (market still prices on model narratives)
2. Affinity-resin/chromatography consumables for AAV separation (hidden chokepoint)
3. GMP capacity & regulatory time, not discovery, kills therapies — capital floods discovery
4. Sequencing is NOT scarce ($100 genome); value moved to interpretation + DNA synthesis/writing
5. Delivery IP licensing as a tax on the whole mRNA/editing field

## Confidence
High: in-vivo editing approval 2027, sequencing commoditized, GMP/data scarcities, MCED timeline. Medium: AI drugs beating baseline (verdict 2027–30). Low/hype: longevity efficacy, precision-fermentation parity, MCED clinical utility. Central caution: bet the bottleneck (data, delivery, GMP, tools), not the molecule.
