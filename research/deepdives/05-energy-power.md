# Energy & Power Systems — Deep Dive (Wave 1)

**Core thesis:** Power is THE binding constraint on AI/electrification. Investable scarcities are NOT generation tech — they're the equipment supply chain, fuel cycle, and skilled labor any new generation must pass through. Deepest moats: large power transformers, gas turbine slots, HALEU, HVDC cable, journeyman electricians. These bind FIRST (2026–2030), longest lead times (3–8 yrs), hardest to substitute. SMRs/fusion/EGS arrive AFTER the bottleneck window (optionality, not relief).

## Structural scarcities (ranked how-soon × how-hard-to-relieve)
1. **Large power transformers & GSUs** — binds NOW, hardest moat. Lead 128–144 wks, some 4 yrs; ~30% fleet shortfall. Expressions: Hitachi Energy (6501.T), **Eaton (ETN)**, Siemens Energy (ENR.DE), GE Vernova, ABB, Schneider; grain-oriented electrical steel.
2. **Gas turbine slots** — binds now–2030, 3-OEM oligopoly. GE Vernova sold out through 2030 (~$150B backlog). **GE Vernova (GEV)**, Siemens Energy (ENR.DE), Mitsubishi Heavy (7011.T).
3. **HALEU/enrichment** — binds 2027–2032; could GATE the SMR rollout. Russia/China only scale producers; US ban. **Centrus (LEU)** only US-owned HALEU; Cameco (CCJ); Urenco/Orano (private).
4. **Uranium upstream** — structural deficit across decade; LT ~$90/lb (14-yr high). **Cameco (CCJ)**, Kazatomprom (KAP.L), Sprott Physical (U.UN/SRUUF), NexGen (NXE), Denison (DNN).
5. **Skilled electrical labor** — NON-CONSENSUS, binds NOW. Microsoft's Brad Smith: "#1 problem." 45–70% of DC construction cost. Barely investable directly → EPCs: **Quanta (PWR)**, MYR (MYRG), Comfort Systems (FIX), EMCOR (EME), MasTec (MTZ), Sterling (STRL).
6. **HVDC & HV cable** — binds 2025–2029, vessel-constrained, >5yr waitlists. **Prysmian (PRY.MI)**, Nexans (NEX.PA), NKT.
7. **Switchgear (MV/HV)** — binds 2025–2027, easing fastest. Eaton (ETN), Schneider (SU.PA), ABB.
8. **Copper** — binds LATE (2030–2035) but most durably. IEA: 30% deficit by 2035. Freeport (FCX), Southern Copper (SCCO), Ivanhoe, Teck.
9. **Interconnection queue/grid access** — soft bottleneck ~2 TW stuck; drives behind-the-meter gas. Bloom (BE), Cummins (CMI), Generac (GNRC).

## Non-consensus (under-priced)
Skilled electricians (#1 per Microsoft, barely investable directly). Transformers/GSUs (true single point of failure). HALEU (gates SMR thesis). HVDC cable + cable-laying vessels. Interconnection-closed grid as the driver of behind-the-meter gas.

## Bear case respected
30–50% of planned 2026 US DC builds at risk; "there will be an overbuild" (Nadella); token costs fell ~280× in 2 yrs. BUT bottleneck is cumulative under-investment in MANUFACTURING capacity, not just AI; even 30% haircut leaves equipment tight (grid-mod + electrification + reshoring demand). Asymmetry favors equipment/fuel/labor chokepoints over generation-tech bets.
