## TAILORED ALLOCATION — $1.5mm sleeve ($800k IRA + $700k taxable)
*(~29% of a $5.2mm portfolio. The full per-name dollar deployment calendar lives in [`POSITION-SIZING.md`](POSITION-SIZING.md) — this section is the allocation; that file is the schedule.)*

**Governing principle (asset location):** with **more IRA room ($800k) than taxable ($700k)**, push everything you'll trim/rebalance — the cyclical single names *and* the cyclical ETFs — into the **IRA** (tax-free turnover). Reserve **taxable** for only the lowest-turnover anchors **plus foreign dividend payers** (ASML, Siemens Energy) so you can claim the foreign-tax credit. Keep US-domiciled single names in the IRA (foreign withholding is unrecoverable there).

*Note: ~29% in one thematic sleeve is aggressive — it only works because the other ~$3.7mm is your diversified ballast (broad index + bonds). Don't let the sleeve creep above ~30% ($1.56mm).*

## How to get in (execution plan)

**1. Phasing — don't lump-sum the whole thing.**
- **Deploy ~half of the sleeve NOW** into the *forward-cheap + backlog-protected* names that don't need a de-rating: **MU, GEV, EME, an IPP, ASML, PAVE.**
- **Dollar-cost-average the crowded/cyclical names over 6–12 months** (monthly tranches): **COPX, NUKZ, SMH, MP, LEU** — these are at high entries, so average through the volatility.
- **Hold the 8–10% dry powder** for the trigger.

**2. The dry-powder trigger (so "wait" never becomes permanent).** Deploy the cash sleeve into the good crowded chokepoints (ASML, GEV, copper) when **either**:
- the 2027–28 air-pocket hits → a ≥20–25% drawdown in the complex, **or**
- the bull case confirms → **two consecutive quarters of hyperscaler capex guided *up*** (the Barclays "$225B too low" scenario) — in which case deploy immediately; don't keep waiting for a dip.

**3. Mechanics.**
- Any major brokerage (Schwab, Fidelity, IBKR). ASML is US-listed; foreign names via US ADRs (Siemens Energy **SMNEY**, Lynas **LYSDY**) or IBKR for direct ASX/Frankfurt listings.
- Hold the most volatile cyclicals (MU, COPX, miners, LEU) and any frequent rebalancing in **tax-advantaged accounts (IRA/401k)** if available; long-term holds (PAVE, GRID, FIW) anywhere.

**4. Risk rules.**
- Cap any single stock at **~8% of the sleeve**; cap the whole sleeve at your Step-0 number.
- **Rebalance** to targets annually, or trim any name that doubles from entry AND re-rates above ~50x forward.
- Predefine your sell discipline before buying — these are cyclicals, not forever-holds.

**5. Stated assumptions / what breaks this.** The whole sleeve implicitly bets that rates don't spike and the dollar doesn't surge, and that there's no near-term hard recession. In any of those, the Tier-1 cyclicals fall together regardless of scarcity. Size for that.
