# Space Economy & Orbital Infrastructure — Deep Dive (Wave 1)

**Core thesis:** Launch cost (current puck) is on a glide path to commoditization (Starship/New Glenn). Next scarcities are downstream physical inputs that DON'T ride reusability curves. Orbital data centers & SBSP are real demand signals but overstated on timeline by ~5–15 yrs (collide with thermal physics + same scarce inputs).

## Roadmap
- 2026: Starship V3 ramp; New Glenn reuse; propellant-transfer demo.
- ~2028–2030: credible window for $100–300/kg IF 10–20 reuses w/ reliability (gating variable for everything).
- Mega-constellations: ~7,000 Starlink ops; Kuiper, China Guowang/Qianfan ramping. ITU "bring-into-use" deadlines force deploy-or-forfeit 2026–2030.
- Orbital DCs: <15% viable by 2029–31, 30–40% by 2032–35. Demos only now (single H100s).
- SBSP: utility-scale a 2040s story.

## Structural scarcities (ranked)
1. **Space-grade power: GaAs/III-V solar cells + gallium** — binds 2027–29. China ~98% gallium/60% germanium (export valve). Space-cell output single-digit MW/yr, >$200/W. Moat HIGH. Expressions: Rocket Lab (RKLB, via SolAero/STARRAY), Boeing (Spectrolab), gallium/Ge ex-China recovery.
2. **Thermal rejection (radiators)** — PHYSICS floor (100–350 W/m²), never eases. The non-consensus true chokepoint on orbital compute. No merchant market — buried in primes. Mostly un-investable publicly (short-the-hype signal).
3. **Rad-hard/qualified electronics + test-beam time** — binds now, tightens to 2028. >52wk lead, 6–12mo qual backlog. Expressions: Microchip (MCHP), BAE, Frontgrade (private), STMicro (STM).
4. **Optical inter-satellite links (OISL)** — binds now–2027, eases 2028+. Talent-bound. Expressions: Rocket Lab (RKLB, owns Mynaric), CACI, Airbus (Tesat).
5. **Ground/downlink capacity** — binds 2026–2030; DSN already constrained. Cailabs, Space Compass, KSAT (private), AWS (AMZN).
6. **Spectrum & orbital slots** — binds now–2030, PERMANENT entrenchment moat. SpaceX (private), Kuiper (AMZN), Eutelsat/OneWeb (ETL.PA).
7. **Noble-gas propellant (xenon→krypton)** — intermittent 2025–2030, HIGH substitution. Linde (LIN), Air Liquide.
8. **Pad throughput & launch licensing** — binds 2026–2030. Accrues to incumbents (SpaceX private, Blue Origin).
9. **Mid-career engineering human capital** — most underpriced cross-cutting; caps every layer's ramp. ~15% attrition. No clean public play.

## Non-consensus
Thermal rejection is the TRUE binding constraint on orbital compute, not launch cost — un-investable publicly (buried in primes). Secondary: gallium/space-grade-cell capacity. Orbital DC & SBSP timelines overstated.
